Greenland Flora & Fauna

Life thriving at the edge of the Arctic world

Fauna

Greenland Wolf (Canis lupus orion)

The Greenland wolf is a rare species once believed extinct by 2002. Today only 40–100 remain, feeding mainly on musk oxen and caribou.

Northern Collared Lemming

Greenland’s only rodent, vital to the tundra food web. Its population cycles support Arctic foxes and snowy owls.

Polar Bear

Apex predator of the Arctic. Adults hunt seals while foxes follow them to scavenge leftovers.

Musk Ox

Known as Umimmak, meaning “the long-bearded,” musk oxen shed their thick winter coats in spring.

Greenland Shark

Also called Eqalussuaq, this slow-moving shark inhabits icy northern waters and is known for extreme longevity.

Peregrine Falcon

The fastest animal on Earth, reaching speeds of up to 320 km/h during dives.

Arctic Bumblebee

A thick-haired bee forming year-round colonies in moss mounds across Greenland.

Flora

Lichens

A symbiotic partnership between fungi and algae that allows survival in extreme Arctic conditions.

Dwarf Fireweed

Greenland’s national flower, known as niviarsiaq, eaten by Inuit communities.

Northern Green Orchid

A small orchid growing in Arctic bogs and wetlands.

Arctous rubra

A low-growing shrub native to cold northern regions.
